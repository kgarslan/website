Workshop - The Echo of Ancestors: The Reemergence and Claims of 'Ancient' Religions in Europe

Interdisciplinary Workshop on the Reconstruction of Ancient Religions in Contemporary Europe, 2025

We are pleased to invite scholars to submit abstracts for the interdisciplinary workshop “Reconstruction of Ancient Religions in Contemporary Europe,” which will be held on April 3–4, 2025. This workshop will bring together researchers from sociology, anthropology, history, and legal and political sciences to explore the reconstruction of ancient religions across Europe.

As emerging religious trends gain recognition, the political and legal processes involved in their acknowledgment are becoming critical. This workshop aims to analyze these dynamics within the context of contemporary European societal structures, focusing on how religious revival is shaped, disseminated, and recognized. We will also examine the political and social challenges that these phenomena raise.

In particular, we are interested in examining belief systems beyond monotheistic traditions, such as polytheism, paganism, and animist traditions, and their role in current debates around identity, culture, and nationhood. This workshop will provide a platform for European researchers to engage in a cross-disciplinary reflection on the implications of these religious movements.

Over the course of two days, our invited specialists will explore topics such as:
✅ Institutional recognition of ancient religions
✅ The intersection of neo-paganism and contemporary social issues
✅ The political and legal dimensions of religious revival

With distinguished scholars and dynamic discussions, this workshop will offer a comprehensive and comparative perspective on the political, legal, and social implications of these movements.

📅 Date: April 3-4, 2025
