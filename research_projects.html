History, Memory, and Identification

History, Memory, and Identification: An Ethnographic Study of the Alsatian Population of Turkish Origin

Project Lead: Kerem Görkem ARSLAN

Scientific Committee:

  • Samim AKGÖNÜL, University of Strasbourg
  • Stéphane DE TAPIA, University of Strasbourg

This study aims to establish an updated research foundation on the diverse communities that shape contemporary Alsace, enrich museum collections, and contribute to the development of museographic elements for the future visitor experience at the Musée Alsacien.

Focusing on the Alsatian population of Turkish origin, our approach seeks to deepen the analysis of migration to France, as well as the experiences of generations born, educated, and socialized in Alsace within this migratory context. We explore the underlying motivations driving this phenomenon while assessing the integration process within the Alsatian community, particularly for non-migrant generations. A key aspect of our field research is the special attention given to specific dates and celebrations, viewing these moments as significant milestones for participant observation in this study.
